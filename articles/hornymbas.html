Why MBAs Are the Horniest People on LinkedIn

The Platform of “Professional Thirst”

LinkedIn is supposed to be a place for jobs, recruiting, and sharing that one time you learned “teamwork.” But in reality, it’s become a playground for MBAs to peacock, humblebrag, and flirt with the algorithm like it owes them dinner.

Nothing screams please validate me quite like a 27-year-old ex-McKinsey associate posting a selfie at 6 a.m. with the caption:
“Gym. Grind. Gratitude. 3G I live by. #mindset #MBAjourney”

The Horny Is Not Always Sexual

No, they’re not out here posting shirtless pics — although that might honestly be less cringe. This is a different kind of thirst. A professional, algorithm-optimized thirst. They're horny for attention. For status. For that sweet, sweet “Top Voice in Business Strategy” badge.

Every post is a pitch. Every comment is a networking attempt. Even their humblebrags are spreadsheets in disguise.

Sample Post, Decoded

Post: “So humbled to announce that I’ve joined Bain as a summer associate after an incredible journey of growth and imposter syndrome.”
Translation: “I’m better than you. Worship me.”

Post: “Got rejected 17 times before getting into Wharton. Never stop believing.”
Translation: “Now that I’m at Wharton, I will never shut up about it.”

The MBA-to-Influencer Pipeline

Somewhere between B-School orientation and internship recruiting, many MBAs undergo a terrifying transformation: they become content creators. Slides become carousels. Cold emails become inspirational threads. Every moment becomes a “lesson.”

They’re out here treating LinkedIn like it’s Instagram in a business suit. But instead of thirst traps, it’s TED Talk energy in Canva templates.

Let Them Cook… Just Not on My Feed

To be fair, not all MBAs are like this. Just a statistically significant number of them. And hey — maybe it’s not their fault. Maybe they were told this is how you "build a personal brand."

But until LinkedIn adds a “mute MBA” filter, I’ll be here, scrolling past the 3-paragraph odes to “resilience” after dropping a Starbucks coffee on their laptop and still making it to the 9 a.m. Zoom.

In conclusion: stay horny, stay humble. But maybe — just maybe — get off LinkedIn.
